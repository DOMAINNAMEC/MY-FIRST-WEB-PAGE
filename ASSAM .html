ABOUT ASSAM

Assam is a state in northeastern India, known for its rich cultural heritage, diverse ethnic communities, and scenic beauty. Here are some key aspects about Assam:
  • Geography and Location:
    • Assam is bordered by Bhutan and Arunachal Pradesh to the north, Nagaland and Manipur to the east, Meghalaya, Tripura, Mizoram, and Bangladesh to the south, and West Bengal to the west. The state is known for its lush green landscapes, including the Brahmaputra River, which flows through the state, and its fertile plains.
  • Economy:
    • Assam is famous for its tea production, contributing to a significant portion of the worldâ€™s tea supply. The state has vast tea gardens, particularly in the regions of Dibrugarh and Tinsukia. Oil and natural gas are also key industries in Assam, with Digboi being one of the oldest oil refineries in the world. Agriculture plays a significant role in Assam's economy, with rice being the staple food. Other important crops include jute, sugarcane, and fruits.
  • Culture:
    • Assam has a vibrant cultural heritage, marked by traditional dance forms such as Bihu, which is associated with the state's major festival of the same name. The state is home to several ethnic groups, including Assamese, Bodos, and various indigenous communities, making it culturally diverse. Assamese literature has a rich history, with well-known poets and authors contributing to Indian literature. The Assamese language is widely spoken along with other local dialects.
  • Tourism:
    • Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is famous for the one-horned rhinoceros and diverse wildlife, making it a major tourist attraction. Manas National Park and Majuli, one of the world's largest river islands, are also popular tourist spots. Sualkuchi is known as the weaving village of Assam and is famous for Assam silk.
  • History:
    • Assam has a rich history, with the Ahom dynasty ruling the region for over 600 years until the British colonization. The state's name "Assam" is derived from "Ahom," a dynasty that introduced advanced military tactics and administration.
  • Politics and Governance:
    • Assam has a significant place in Indian politics, with issues related to immigration, ethnic diversity, and autonomy being central to its political landscape. Disputes over borders with neighboring states like Nagaland and Meghalaya, as well as the Assam Accord related to illegal immigration, are important political topics in the state.
  • Festivals:
    • Apart from Bihu, which celebrates the agricultural season, Assam also celebrates festivals like Durga Puja, Ali-Ai-Ligang (by the Mishing community), and Baishagu (by the Bodos). Assam is a land where nature, culture, and history come together, making it one of the most diverse and beautiful states in India.

DISTRICTS OF ASSAM

Assam is divided into several districts, each with its own unique culture, geography, and significance. As of now, Assam has 35 districts. Here's a list of the districts in Assam:
  1. Baksa
    • Located in the Bodoland Territorial Region (BTR).
    • Known for its natural beauty and wildlife sanctuaries.
  2. Barpeta
    • Famous for the Barpeta Satra (Vaishnavite monastery).
    • An important cultural and religious center.
  3. Biswanath
    • Home to the Biswanath Temple on the banks of the Brahmaputra River.
    • A newly created district.
  4. Bongaigaon
    • Known for the Bongaigaon Refinery, one of India's oldest oil refineries.
    • A blend of urban and rural Assam.
  5. Cachar
    • Silchar, the district headquarters, is the cultural and economic center of southern Assam.
    • Rich in tea gardens
  6. Charaideo
    • Holds historical significance as the ancient capital of the Ahom dynasty
    • Contains the burial mounds or "Maidams" of Ahom kings.
  7. Chirang
    • Part of the Bodoland Territorial Region (BTR).
    • Known for its rich biodiversity.
  8. . Darrang
    • Mangaldai is the main town.
    • Known for agriculture and its ancient temples.
  9. Dhemaji
    • Known for its rural charm and as a flood-prone area due to the Brahmaputra River..
    • Rich in agriculture.
  10. Dhubri
    • Known for the historical Sikarganj and the Gurdwara Sri Guru Tegh Bahadur Sahib.
    • A key center of Islamic and Sikh heritage in Assam.
  11. Dibrugarh
    • Known as the "Tea City of India."
    • Dibrugarh is also an educational and industrial hub.
  12. Dima Hasao
    • Also known as North Cachar Hills.
    • Known for its hilly terrain and cultural diversity.
  13. Goalpara
    • Famous for Surya Pahar, an archaeological and religious site.
    • Known for folk traditions like "Goalpariya Lokgeet."
  14. . Golaghat
    • Home to the famous Kaziranga National Park.
    • An agricultural district with historical significance.
  15. Hailakandi
    • Known for its tea gardens.
    • Culturally rich, with a blend of Bengali and Assamese influences.
  16. Hojai
    • A newly created district, known for its large production of Agarwood.
    • Agriculture is a major occupation.
  17. Jorhat
    • Known as the "Cultural Capital of Assam."
    • Rich in tea estates and the location of the Tocklai Tea Research Institute.
  18. Kamrup (Metro)
    • Largest district in Assam by area.
    • Known for its rich tribal culture and scenic beauty.
  19. Kamrup (Rural)
    • Surrounds the metro area but has its own identity with rural landscapes and traditional Assamese culture.
  20. Karbi Anglong
    • Largest district in Assam by area.
    • Known for its rich tribal culture and scenic beauty.
  21. Karimganj
    • Shares a border with Bangladesh.
    • Known for its wetlands and agricultural land.
  22. Kokrajhar
    • Part of the Bodoland Territorial Region (BTR).
    • Known for its wildlife, including the Manas National Park.
  23. Lakhimpur
    • Known for its rice fields and rural life.
    • Situated on the northern bank of the Brahmaputra River.
  24. Majuli
    • The world's largest river island, located in the Brahmaputra River.
    • A cultural hub for Assamese Vaishnavism.
  25. Morigaon
    • Famous for the Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary, which has the highest density of one-horned rhinos.
    • An agricultural and pilgrimage cent
  26. Nagaon
    • Known for its historical figures and being the birthplace of Srimanta Sankardev, a key figure in Assamese culture.
    • Rich in agriculture.
  27. Nalbari
    • Known for its traditional festivals and the Billeshwar Temple.
    • An agricultural district.
  28. Sivasagar
    • A historical district, known for its Ahom monuments like Rang Ghar and Talatal Ghar.
    • A center for oil production.
  29. Sonitpur
    • Famous for its rich wildlife, including the Nameri National Park.
    • Tezpur is the district headquarters, known for its cultural heritage.
  30. South Salmara-Mankachar
    • Border district near Bangladesh.
    • Known for its picturesque landscape along the Brahmaputra River.
  31. Tinsukia
    • Known for its tea gardens and as an industrial center.
    • The town of Digboi, famous for its oil fields, is located here.
  32. Tamulpur
    • A newly formed district carved out of Baksa.
    • Part of the Bodoland Territorial Region (BTR).
  33. Udalguri
    • Located in the Bodoland Territorial Region (BTR).
    • Known for its scenic beauty and the Orang National Park.
  34. West Karbi Anglong
    • A newly formed district, carved out of Karbi Anglong.
    • Rich in hilly terrain and cultural diversity.
  35. Bajali
    • A newly created district, carved out of Barpeta.
    • Known for its rural charm and cultural heritage.

These districts are diverse, both in terms of geography and culture, making Assam a land of great natural and cultural richness.
